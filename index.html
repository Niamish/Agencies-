§1

What I build for agencies

Your studio sells more design than it can build. I'm the dev who takes the sold work and ships it — pixel-perfect to the Figma, with the animation and polish layer most white-label developers can't deliver.

  • Figma-to-code conversion — landing pages and marketing sites, faithful to the comp down to letter-spacing.
  • Motion & premium polish — scroll choreography, kinetic type, micro-interactions. The layer that makes clients say "this feels expensive."
  • React component builds — clean, documented components your team can extend without calling me.
  • Rescue & polish passes — an existing build that's 80% there and needs the last 20% done properly.

Best fit

  • Studios selling premium websites but short on frontend capacity.
  • Founders or creative directors who need a reliable production lane.
  • Teams that care about responsive polish, motion restraint, and clean handoff.

Not a fit

  • Unscoped "make it pop" builds with no design direction or decision owner.
  • Backend-heavy products, complex auth, or long-running product engineering.
  • Rush work that requires skipping QA, mobile passes, or handoff notes.

§3

How a build runs

  1. Brief

    Figma link, copy, and any reference notes. A Loom walkthrough helps but isn't required. I confirm scope and a fixed quote within hours, not days.

  2. Build on staging

    Live staging URL from day one. You and your client-facing team watch real progress — no Figma-to-handoff gap, no surprise reveals.

  3. Revision passes

    Two rounds included. Notes via email, Slack, or annotated screenshots — whatever your team already uses.

  4. Handoff

    Clean repo or zip, deployment to your hosting if needed, and a short written handover. Code ownership transfers to you on payment, in full.

§4

White-label terms

  • Your brand on everything. No credits, no watermarks, no signature in the source. As far as your client knows, your studio built it.
  • Your client stays yours. I never contact your client, and I don't take referrals from your projects. Ever.
  • NDA-standard by default. Happy to sign yours; project details are never discussed publicly either way.
  • Portfolio use only with written permission. Default is none.
  • Full IP transfer on payment. No licensing tails, no dependencies on me after handoff.
